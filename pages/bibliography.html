Primary Sources

Court Records and Legal Documents

  • Mahon v. Justice, 127 U.S. 700. 1888. United States Supreme Court.

    Supreme Court case that ruled on the legality of Kentucky's extradition of Hatfield family members from West Virginia.

  • Commonwealth of Kentucky v. Ellison Mounts. 1890. Pike County Circuit Court Records, Pike County Courthouse, Pikeville, Kentucky.

    Trial records for Ellison Mounts, who was convicted and executed for his role in the feud.

  • Logan County Land Deeds, 1870-1891. Logan County Courthouse, Logan, West Virginia.

    Property records documenting Hatfield family land holdings.

  • Pike County Land Deeds, 1865-1891. Pike County Courthouse, Pikeville, Kentucky.

    Property records documenting McCoy family land holdings.

Contemporary Newspaper Accounts (1863-1891)

  • "The Hatfield-McCoy Vendetta." Louisville Courier-Journal, January 10, 1888.

  • "The Kentucky Feud: More About the Hatfield-McCoy Vendetta." New York Times, January 25, 1888.

  • "The Hatfield Gang." Wheeling Intelligencer, January 11, 1888.

  • "The Hatfield War." Huntington Advertiser, February 2, 1888.

  • "Trial of the Hatfields." Louisville Courier-Journal, August 15, 1889.

Personal Correspondence and Diaries

  • Hatfield, William Anderson. Letter to Governor E. Willis Wilson. January 18, 1888. West Virginia State Archives, Charleston, West Virginia.

  • McCoy, Randolph. Personal diary, 1880-1885. McCoy Family Collection, University of Kentucky Special Collections, Lexington, Kentucky.

  • Phillips, Frank. Letters to Pike County Sheriff, 1887-1888. Eastern Kentucky University Special Collections, Richmond, Kentucky.

Government Documents

  • United States Census Records. 1860, 1870, 1880, 1890. Pike County, Kentucky and Logan County, West Virginia.

  • West Virginia Governor's Papers. Correspondence regarding the Hatfield-McCoy Feud, 1887-1890. West Virginia State Archives, Charleston, West Virginia.

  • Kentucky Governor's Papers. Correspondence regarding the Hatfield-McCoy Feud, 1887-1890. Kentucky Department for Libraries and Archives, Frankfort, Kentucky.

Citation Notes

All sources in this bibliography are cited according to the Chicago Manual of Style, 17th edition. For assistance with creating your own citations, please use our Citation Generator.

Some primary sources, particularly newspaper articles from the 1880s, contain language and perspectives that reflect the biases and prejudices of their time. These sources are included for their historical value, but their inclusion does not constitute an endorsement of these views.

This bibliography is regularly updated as new sources are incorporated into the database. Last updated: April 2025.
