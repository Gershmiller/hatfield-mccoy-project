Hatfield Territory

Devil Anse Hatfield's Cabin

Hatfield Family Home

Home of William Anderson "Devil Anse" Hatfield and headquarters of the Hatfield family during the feud. Located in Logan County, West Virginia (now Mingo County), this cabin served as the center of Hatfield operations.

Location Logan County, WV (now Mingo County)
Significance Hatfield Family Headquarters

Hatfield Cemetery

Burial Ground

Final resting place of Devil Anse Hatfield, marked by a life-sized marble statue, and many other Hatfield family members. The cemetery is located on a hillside in Logan County, West Virginia.

Location Sarah Ann, WV
Notable Feature Life-sized marble statue of Devil Anse

Mate Creek

Settlement Area

Area of significant Hatfield family settlement, later known as Matewan. This was an important crossing point between territories and a center of Hatfield influence.

Location Mingo County, WV
Significance Hatfield Settlement

McCoy Territory

Randolph McCoy's Cabin

McCoy Family Home

Site of the New Year's Night Raid of 1888, where the Hatfields burned the McCoy home and killed two of Randolph's children. Located in Pike County, Kentucky, this was the primary residence of the McCoy family.

Location Pike County, KY
Significance Site of 1888 New Year's Night Raid

McCoy Cemetery

Burial Ground

Burial place of Randolph McCoy, his wife Sarah, and several of their children who died during the feud. Located in Pike County, Kentucky, this cemetery contains the graves of many McCoy family members.

Location Pike County, KY
Notable Burials Randolph and Sarah McCoy

Pikeville, Kentucky

County Seat

County seat of Pike County where several trials related to the feud took place, including the trial of Cotton Top Mounts. This town was a center of McCoy support and legal proceedings against the Hatfields.

Location Pike County, KY
Significance Site of feud-related trials

Conflict Sites

Pawpaw Creek

Execution Site

Site where three McCoy brothers (Tolbert, Pharmer, and Bud) were executed by the Hatfields in 1882. This location along Pawpaw Creek was the scene of one of the most violent episodes of the feud.

Location Kentucky-West Virginia border
Date August 9, 1882

Blackberry Creek

Dispute Location

Area where the dispute over a pig—often cited as the initial spark of the feud—took place in 1878. This creek area was the site of the contested ownership of a pig between Floyd Hatfield and Randolph McCoy.

Location Pike County, KY
Date 1878

Election Grounds

Public Gathering Site

Site where Johnse Hatfield met Roseanna McCoy in 1880 and where Ellison Hatfield was fatally stabbed in 1882. This public gathering place was the scene of both romance and violence during the feud.

Location Pike County, KY
Significance Site of multiple key feud events

Tug Fork River

Boundary

The river that forms the boundary between Kentucky and West Virginia, serving as both a physical and jurisdictional boundary between the families. This natural border complicated legal proceedings during the feud.

Location KY-WV Border
Significance State boundary between families

Modern Sites

Hatfield-McCoy Trails

Modern Attraction

Modern trail system passing through many historic feud sites, established in 2000 for recreational use. These trails allow visitors to experience the terrain where the feud took place.

Location Southern West Virginia
Established 2000

Hatfield-McCoy Museum

Historical Museum

Museum housing artifacts, photographs, and documents related to the famous feud. This modern museum preserves the history of the feud and educates visitors about its significance.

Location Williamson, WV
Features Artifacts, documents, photographs

Hatfield-McCoy House Inn

Tourist Accommodation

Historic building converted into a themed bed and breakfast catering to tourists interested in the feud. This inn allows visitors to stay in a location connected to the feud's history.

Location Williamson, WV
Type Bed and Breakfast
