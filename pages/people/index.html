Key Figures of the Feud

Explore the lives and roles of the central figures in the Hatfield-McCoy feud, from family patriarchs to peripheral characters who influenced the conflict.

Family Patriarchs

William Anderson "Devil Anse" Hatfield

Hatfield Family Patriarch (1839-1921)

The leader of the Hatfield clan during the feud, Devil Anse was a timber merchant and former Confederate captain. Known for his fierce temperament and skilled marksmanship, he eventually found religion later in life and was baptized at age 73.

Born September 9, 1839
Died January 6, 1921
Spouse Levicy Chafin
Children 13

Randolph "Ole Ran'l" McCoy

McCoy Family Patriarch (1825-1914)

The leader of the McCoy clan, Randolph was a farmer and ferry operator. He lost five of his children to the violence of the feud, including three sons who were tied to pawpaw bushes and executed by the Hatfields in 1882.

Born October 30, 1825
Died March 28, 1914
Spouse Sarah "Sally" McCoy
Children 16

Key Hatfield Family Members

William Anderson "Cap" Hatfield II

Second son of Devil Anse (1864-1930)

Known as the most dangerous of Devil Anse's sons, Cap was a skilled marksman who participated in many of the violent episodes of the feud.

Johnson "Johnse" Hatfield

Eldest son of Devil Anse (1862-1922)

His romance with Roseanna McCoy in 1880 is often cited as one of the catalysts for the escalation of the feud. Later married Roseanna's cousin Nancy McCoy.

Levicy Chafin Hatfield

Wife of Devil Anse (1842-1929)

The matriarch of the Hatfield family who stood by her husband throughout the feud while raising their 13 children.

Ellison Hatfield

Brother of Devil Anse (1841-1882)

His murder by three McCoy brothers during an election day dispute in 1882 led to their subsequent execution by the Hatfields, dramatically escalating the feud.

Ellison "Cotton Top" Mounts

Illegitimate son of Ellison Hatfield (1865-1890)

Known for his white-blond hair and diminished mental capacity, he was executed by hanging for his role in the murder of Alifair McCoy during the New Year's Night Raid.

Jim Vance

Uncle of Devil Anse (1832-1888)

A violent and ruthless man who was one of the leaders of the New Year's Night Raid on the McCoy cabin. He was later killed by Frank Phillips in 1888.

Key McCoy Family Members

Sarah "Sally" McCoy

Wife of Randolph McCoy (1829-1890)

The matriarch of the McCoy family who was severely beaten during the New Year's Night Raid of 1888 and never fully recovered from her injuries.

Roseanna McCoy

Daughter of Randolph McCoy (1859-1889)

Her romance with Johnse Hatfield in 1880 is often romanticized as a "Romeo and Juliet" story. After being abandoned by Johnse while pregnant, she died of a broken heart at age 29.

Tolbert McCoy

Son of Randolph McCoy (1854-1882)

One of the three McCoy brothers who killed Ellison Hatfield in 1882 and were subsequently executed by the Hatfields while tied to pawpaw bushes.

Pharmer McCoy

Son of Randolph McCoy (1863-1882)

One of the three McCoy brothers executed by the Hatfields in 1882 for the killing of Ellison Hatfield during an election day dispute.

Randolph "Bud" McCoy Jr.

Son of Randolph McCoy (1864-1882)

The youngest of the three McCoy brothers executed by the Hatfields in 1882, he was only 17 years old at the time of his death.

Alifair McCoy

Daughter of Randolph McCoy (1858-1888)

Shot and killed by Cap Hatfield or Cotton Top Mounts during the New Year's Night Raid on the McCoy cabin in 1888.

Other Key Figures

Perry Cline

Attorney and McCoy ally (1849-1891)

A lawyer and politician who lost a land dispute to Devil Anse Hatfield. He later used his political connections to bring in bounty hunters against the Hatfields.

Frank Phillips

Bounty Hunter (1862-1898)

Also known as "Bad Frank," he was a deputy and bounty hunter who captured several Hatfields for trial in Kentucky. He killed Jim Vance and later married Nancy McCoy after her divorce from Johnse Hatfield.

Wall Hatfield

Brother of Devil Anse (1835-1890)

A more moderate member of the Hatfield family who served as a justice of the peace. Despite claiming innocence, he was convicted and died in prison for his alleged role in the feud.

Nancy McCoy

Cousin of Roseanna McCoy (1859-1927)

Married Johnse Hatfield in 1881 after his relationship with her cousin Roseanna ended. She later divorced him and married Frank Phillips.

Valentine "Uncle Wall" Hatfield

Brother of Devil Anse (1834-1890)

A justice of the peace who tried to remain neutral during the feud but was eventually arrested, convicted, and died in prison.

Bill Staton

Related to both families (d. 1880)

Married to a Hatfield and brother-in-law to two McCoys, he was killed by Sam and Paris McCoy in 1880 following his testimony in the pig dispute trial.

Mid-Century Legacy Figures (1940s-1950s)

Shirley Hatfield

Great-granddaughter of Devil Anse (1922-2010)

Featured in the 1944 Life magazine article working alongside Frankie McCoy in a uniform factory during World War II, symbolizing the reconciliation between the families.

Frankie McCoy

Great-granddaughter of Randolph McCoy (1923-2008)

Worked alongside Shirley Hatfield during World War II, their cooperation symbolizing how the next generations had moved beyond the feud that divided their ancestors.
