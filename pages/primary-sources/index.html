Primary Sources

Explore original documents, newspaper articles, court records, and other primary sources that provide direct evidence about the Hatfield-McCoy feud.

Newspaper Articles

Louisville Courier-Journal Article, 1888

January 25, 1888

Article reporting on the New Year's Night Raid on the McCoy cabin, describing the attack and the deaths of Alifair and Calvin McCoy. This article helped bring national attention to the feud.

Source Type Newspaper Article
Publication Louisville Courier-Journal

Wheeling Intelligencer Article, 1882

August 24, 1882

Article reporting on the execution of the three McCoy brothers by the Hatfields. This early report provides details about the killing and initial reactions from the community.

Source Type Newspaper Article
Publication Wheeling Intelligencer

New York Times Article, 1890

February 19, 1890

Article reporting on the execution of Ellison "Cotton Top" Mounts for his role in the New Year's Night Raid. This national coverage demonstrates how the feud had captured attention beyond Appalachia.

Source Type Newspaper Article
Publication New York Times

Life Magazine Article, 1944

April 21, 1944

Feature article about Shirley Hatfield and Frankie McCoy working together in a uniform factory during World War II, symbolizing the reconciliation between the families. This article includes photographs of the two women.

Source Type Magazine Article
Publication Life Magazine

Legal Documents

Pig Theft Trial Transcript, 1878

October 1878

Court records from the trial regarding Floyd Hatfield's alleged theft of Randolph McCoy's pig. This document includes testimony from Bill Staton, who was later killed for his role in the trial.

Source Type Court Transcript
Location Pike County Courthouse

Kentucky Extradition Request, 1887

September 10, 1887

Official request from Kentucky Governor Simon Buckner to West Virginia Governor E. Willis Wilson for the extradition of Devil Anse Hatfield and others for the murder of the McCoy brothers.

Source Type Government Document
Signatories Governor Simon Buckner

Cotton Top Mounts Trial Records, 1889-1890

December 1889 - February 1890

Court records from the trial of Ellison "Cotton Top" Mounts for the murder of Alifair McCoy during the New Year's Night Raid. These documents include witness testimonies and the final verdict.

Source Type Court Records
Location Pike County Courthouse

U.S. Supreme Court Decision, 1888

May 14, 1888

Supreme Court decision in Mahon v. Justice regarding the kidnapping of several Hatfields from West Virginia to stand trial in Kentucky. This ruling had significant implications for interstate extradition law.

Source Type Legal Decision
Case Number 127 U.S. 700

Personal Correspondence

Letter from Devil Anse Hatfield, 1887

November 26, 1887

Letter written by Devil Anse Hatfield to a local newspaper defending his family's actions and denying responsibility for certain attacks attributed to the Hatfields. This rare document provides insight into Devil Anse's perspective.

Source Type Personal Letter
Author William Anderson Hatfield

Letter from Perry Cline, 1888

January 10, 1888

Letter from Perry Cline to Kentucky Governor Simon Buckner requesting assistance in capturing the Hatfields following the New Year's Night Raid. This document reveals Cline's role in escalating the legal pursuit of the Hatfields.

Source Type Personal Letter
Author Perry Cline

Letter from Randolph McCoy, 1891

March 15, 1891

Letter written by Randolph McCoy reflecting on the feud and its impact on his family. Written after the most violent period of the feud had ended, this letter provides insight into Randolph's perspective on the conflict.

Source Type Personal Letter
Author Randolph McCoy

Photographs

Devil Anse Hatfield and Family, 1888

1888

Photograph of Devil Anse Hatfield with his wife Levicy and several of their children. This rare family portrait provides a visual record of the Hatfield family during the height of the feud.

Source Type Photograph
Photographer Unknown

Cap Hatfield with Rifle, 1895

1895

Photograph of Cap Hatfield, son of Devil Anse, posing with his rifle. Cap was known as one of the most dangerous members of the Hatfield family during the feud.

Source Type Photograph
Photographer Unknown

Randolph McCoy and Family, 1891

1891

Photograph of Randolph McCoy with his wife Sarah and their surviving children. This image was taken after the most violent period of the feud and shows the aging McCoy patriarch.

Source Type Photograph
Photographer Unknown

Shirley Hatfield and Frankie McCoy, 1944

April 1944

Photograph from Life magazine showing Shirley Hatfield and Frankie McCoy working together in a uniform factory during World War II. This image symbolizes the reconciliation between the families.

Source Type Photograph
Photographer Life Magazine Staff

Interviews & Testimonies

Cap Hatfield Interview, 1921

June 15, 1921

Newspaper interview with Cap Hatfield following the death of his father, Devil Anse. In this interview, Cap reflects on the feud and his father's legacy.

Source Type Interview
Publication Huntington Herald-Dispatch

Hatfield-McCoy Descendants Interviews, 1950

August 1950

Collection of interviews with descendants of both families conducted by a folklorist. These interviews provide perspectives on the feud from those who heard about it directly from participants.

Source Type Oral History
Interviewer Leonard Roberts
