Additional Resources

Explore books, websites, and other resources to deepen your understanding of the Hatfield-McCoy feud.

Books & Publications

The Feud: The Hatfields and McCoys: The True Story

By Dean King (2013)

A comprehensive account of the feud that draws on extensive research and interviews with descendants of both families. King provides detailed context about the social, economic, and political factors that contributed to the conflict.

The Hatfields and the McCoys

By Otis K. Rice (1982)

One of the most authoritative accounts of the feud, written by a historian who specialized in Appalachian history. Rice's work is noted for its careful attention to primary sources and historical accuracy.

Blood Feud: The Hatfields and the McCoys

By Lisa Alther (2012)

Alther, who has family connections to the region, combines historical research with personal insights to create a nuanced portrait of the feud and its lasting impact on Appalachian culture.

The Tale of the Devil: The Biography of Devil Anse Hatfield

By Dr. Coleman C. Hatfield and Robert Y. Spence (2003)

Written by a descendant of Devil Anse Hatfield, this biography provides unique insights into the Hatfield patriarch's life and character, drawing on family stories and records.

Historical Sites

Hatfield-McCoy Trails

Southern West Virginia

A system of off-road trails that pass through many historic feud sites. While primarily designed for recreational use, the trails include historical markers and information about the feud at various points.

Official Website

Hatfield & McCoy Dinner Feud

Pigeon Forge, Tennessee

A popular dinner theater show that presents a comedic interpretation of the feud. While not historically accurate, the show has helped keep public interest in the feud alive.

Official Website

Hatfield-McCoy Driving Tour

Kentucky and West Virginia

A self-guided driving tour that takes visitors to key sites related to the feud, including the McCoy well, the Pawpaw Creek execution site, and the Hatfield Cemetery with Devil Anse's statue.

Digital Resources

West Virginia Archives and History

Digital Archive

The West Virginia Archives maintains a collection of documents, photographs, and other materials related to the Hatfield-McCoy feud, many of which have been digitized and are available online.

Visit Archive

PBS American Experience: The Feud

Documentary

This PBS documentary provides a detailed examination of the feud, featuring interviews with historians and descendants of both families. The PBS website includes additional resources and educational materials.

Watch Documentary

History Channel: Hatfields & McCoys

Miniseries

This award-winning 2012 miniseries starring Kevin Costner and Bill Paxton brought renewed attention to the feud. While it takes some dramatic liberties, it provides a compelling visualization of the conflict.

Annual Events

Hatfield-McCoy Reunion Festival

Matewan, West Virginia

An annual festival held in June that celebrates the reconciliation between the families. The event includes music, food, crafts, and historical presentations about the feud.

Hatfield-McCoy Marathon

Williamson, West Virginia

An annual marathon held in June that takes runners through the historic feud territory, crossing between Kentucky and West Virginia. The event includes a full marathon, half marathon, and 5K options.

Official Website
